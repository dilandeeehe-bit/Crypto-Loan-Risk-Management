# Crypto-Loan-Risk-Management
Crypto loan risk management guide. Learn margin calls, liquidation risk, and safe borrowing strategies. CryptaLend prioritizes borrower protection

Borrowing against cryptocurrency introduces both opportunities and risks.

Understanding risk management is essential for borrowers who use digital assets as collateral.

Because cryptocurrency prices can be highly volatile, borrowers must carefully manage collateral levels and loan structures.

## Why Risk Management Matters

When borrowers take a crypto-backed loan, their digital assets are used as collateral.

If the value of that collateral drops significantly, lenders may need to protect themselves from potential losses.

This is why risk management mechanisms such as margin calls and liquidation thresholds exist.

## Margin Calls

A margin call occurs when the collateral value falls below a required level.

In this situation, the borrower may be required to add more collateral or partially repay the loan.

Margin calls help maintain collateral coverage for outstanding loans.

## Liquidation Risk

If the collateral value continues to fall and the borrower does not respond to a margin call, liquidation may occur.

Liquidation involves selling part or all of the collateral to repay the outstanding loan.

Understanding liquidation thresholds is essential before borrowing against cryptocurrency.

## Strategies for Reducing Risk

Borrowers often manage risk by maintaining conservative loan-to-value ratios.

Lower LTV ratios provide a buffer during market volatility and reduce the likelihood of liquidation.

Borrowers may also monitor market conditions and adjust collateral levels when necessary.
